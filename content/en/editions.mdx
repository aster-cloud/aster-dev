# Editions

Aster comes in three editions. The **language and engine are identical** across all
three — the difference is who runs it and what's managed for you.

## At a glance

| | Open Source | Cloud (SaaS) | Enterprise |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Who runs it** | You, on your machine | We, at `aster-lang.cloud` | You, in your VPC/cluster |
| **License** | Apache-2.0 | Subscription | Subscription + perpetual fallback |
| **Best for** | Building, learning, embedding | Teams wanting managed multi-tenant | Regulated, data-residency, air-gapped |
| **Get started** | `npm i @aster-cloud/aster-lang-ts` | [aster-lang.cloud](https://aster-lang.cloud) | Contact sales |

## Language & engine

Every edition ships the full language and both runtimes — nothing is held back at the
language level.

| | OSS | Cloud | Enterprise |
| --- | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| Multi-language CNL (en / 中文 / Deutsch / हिन्दी) | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Java/Truffle reference engine | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| TypeScript engine (browser/Node) | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| LSP / editor extension | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Custom lexicon authoring | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
